04 / Brand & Graphic Design

A System.

Not A Logo.

A brand that looks improvised gets treated like one. Type, color, and usage rules written down once — logos and full brand books, posters, banners, business cards, decks, packaging, and UI/UX, one visual language across all of it.

If every asset needs a new decision, you don't have a brand — you have a folder.

A logo file with no rules around it turns every new asset into a fresh design argument: which blue, which weight, how much padding. Nothing compounds.

→ So Lasha builds the system first — the logo is just the first thing it gets applied to.
