Issue №01 — Strategy & Creative Management

Strategy & Creative Management

Good creative built on the wrong brief is just expensive guessing. Account management, brand positioning, and campaign management run as one thread from pitch to delivery — the audience gets named and the brief gets right before anything is made.

The Argument

Most agencies start with the deliverable. We start with the decision it's supposed to change.

A logo, a reel, a media plan — none of it means anything if nobody agreed what it's for. Teams end up producing work that's technically good and strategically homeless: on-brand, on-brief, off-target.

→ So the account lead is the first hire on every project, not the last review before it ships.

In Detail

  1. 01
    Account Management

    One point of contact across every service you use — briefs, timelines, and revisions run through a single desk instead of getting re-explained to a new freelancer each time.

  2. 02
    Brand Positioning

    Where you sit against competitors, in language specific enough that a designer or copywriter can act on it without guessing what "premium but approachable" means.

  3. 03
    Campaign Management

    A single campaign brief that photography, video, social, and paid media all work from, so a launch doesn't fragment into five unrelated executions of the same idea.

How It Runs

01
The Intake Call

Thirty minutes, no deck. We ask what's actually failing right now, not what package you think you need.

02
The Brief

Tamuna turns the call into a written brief — audience, objective, constraints — and it doesn't move to production until you've signed off on it.

03
Allocation

The right internal team gets assigned against the brief, not against whoever's free — photography and copy don't get handed to the same generalist.

04
The Standing Review

A recurring check-in against the original brief, not a status meeting — so scope drift gets caught while it's still cheap to fix.

Proof Of Concept — The Audit Spread

The format every strategy engagement starts with: a one-page audit of what's currently running against what the brief actually calls for. Once real client work exists, this is where it's shown.

Before — As Found

Three channels, three different voices, no shared brief.

The Audit

Every asset checked against one question: does this serve the objective?

After — Realigned

One brief, one calendar, every channel reading from it.

Investment

Included in every package — strategy isn't a line item you add later, it's how the other eight services stay pointed at the same target.
