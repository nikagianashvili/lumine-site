Issue №01 — Strategy & Creative Management

Strategy & Creative Management

Good creative built on the wrong brief is just expensive guessing. Account management, brand positioning, and campaign management run as one thread from pitch to delivery — the audience gets named and the brief gets right before anything is made.

Twelve sections — the long version, for when "trust us" isn't enough.

Brief №014
Approved For Read

The Diagnosis

Most agencies start with the deliverable. We start with the decision it's supposed to change.

Five signs the brief — not the execution — is the actual problem:

Every channel has a different voice, and nobody planned it that way.
The team can produce work fast but can't say why any specific piece exists.
"Premium but approachable" gets used a lot. Defined never.
A campaign wraps, and the next one starts from zero instead of from what was learned.
The account gets discussed in three separate group chats.

→ So the account lead is the first hire on every project, not the last review before it ships. — Tamuna, Account Lead

The Argument

A logo doesn't have an opinion.

A media plan doesn't know your competitor's next move.

Someone has to hold the whole picture. That's this service.

How It Runs

01 01
The Intake Call

Thirty minutes, no deck. We ask what's actually failing right now, not what package you think you need.

02 02
The Brief

Tamuna turns the call into a written brief — audience, objective, constraints — and it doesn't move to production until you've signed off on it.

03 03
Allocation

The right internal team gets assigned against the brief, not against whoever's free — photography and copy don't get handed to the same generalist.

A recurring check-in against the original brief, not a status meeting — so scope drift gets caught while it's still cheap to fix.
